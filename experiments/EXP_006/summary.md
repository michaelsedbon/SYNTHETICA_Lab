# EXP_006 — Fungal Electrophysiology Literature Review & Characterization

## Status: Active

## Goal

Build the scientific foundation for the Bio Electronic Music project by:
1. Collecting and summarizing the key literature on fungal electrophysiology (spiking, light response)
2. Characterizing the electrical spiking behaviour — especially the blue/UV light-evoked response — from the reference paper (Mishra et al. 2024, *Science Robotics*)

## Reference Paper

**Sensorimotor control of robots mediated by electrophysiological measurements of fungal mycelia**
Mishra, Kim, Baghdadi, Johnson, Hodge & Shepherd (2024). *Science Robotics* 9, eadk8019.
DOI: [10.1126/scirobotics.adk8019](https://doi.org/10.1126/scirobotics.adk8019)

## Key Findings (from seed paper)

- *Pleurotus eryngii* mycelia produce spontaneous action-potential-like spikes (mean ~135 µV, ~0.12 Hz)
- UV light evokes 3–10× amplified spiking (up to 18,569 µV)
- Blue light also evokes response (~83 µV) — red/white do not
- Mechanism: WC-1 blue-light photoreceptor
- Signals recorded stably for 30+ days via extracellular electrodes + PicoLog ADC-24
